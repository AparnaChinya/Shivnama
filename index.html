'OM Prabhave namaH'

We now proceed to the 3rd Nama 'Prabhuh'. Prabhuh means The one who rules. Lord Shiva being the Supreme rules over the Jagat- consisting of both animate & inanimate.

A little background info now... This vast Jagat can be classified into 3 categories.. The inert (inanimate) Jagat, The jivas (souls), Ishvara. Paramatma. The Jagat is inert, limited & prone to change. So it is a Karya as we saw earlier. Now Jiva/soul , i.e myself is an active entity. How? Everyone says I think, I eat, I run,, etc. Also w.r.t his/ her gross body one says I am strong, I am tall, I am fair, etc..W.r.t the subtle body one says I am intelligent, I am happy, I can remember well, etc. Thus the jiva is proved to be not inert. The 3rd entity is Paramatma/God. We by ourselves do not know about his existence, but believe in Him by trust in the words of scriptures, parents, preceptors, etc. His existence as an active entity is proved easily by enquiry in to the creation of this world. Maybe a person can create a mobile phone, an atom bomb, an airplane, etc. But who created the raw material for all these inventions, who created the rivers, mountains, oceans, etc....obviously a super being far more intelligent & capable. In short God can be defined as One who is responsible & capable for which we are not responsible or capable of (definition from Swami Paramanada Bharati ji). Thus with a little more inquiry & reasoning we see that God is far superior to the other two entities. He is a super-set of the other two (jiva & Jagat) which is the matter on hand. We will take to it next....based on Shastra & inquiry.

Continuing our discussion on Shiva sahasranama... Let's revert to the qualities of the jiva for a better understanding. We saw the jiva has a gross body & a subtle body. When we refer to jiva here, it is useful to realise/take Jiva as myself. The gross body called sthoola shareera is the outer motor & sense organs, so also other anatomy of the individual. The subtle body called sookshma sharira consists of the motor & sense faculties, the mind quartet- mind, intellect, memory & ego, the 5 life forces. Remember, the subtle-sookshma sharira is the invisible driving force of the gross body. The discussion gets tougher & more interesting now on.

The sthoola sharira essentially is- the speech, hands, feet/legs, the two excretory organs- the motor organs; the eyes, ears, tongue, nose & skin- the sense organs, etc. The gross & subtle bodies together facilitate all the activity (both external & internal) of the individual.

In each jiva these aspects work to their limits as destined by his/her karma. This now sets the preamble to all future discussions. Also each of these aspects are controlled by individual Devatas also in accordance to his karma. These Devatas exist as Manasa Shariras- similar to the Jiva's subtle/sookshma body. But these Karma Devatas as they can be referred to, are highly evolved beings with wonderful capacities, capable of taking/appearing in any form as they desire. These Devatas also reside in the respective parts of the individual's body (their office) & witness/administer the phala of the jiva starting at birth till the end. It is a long discussion to explain this part of philosophy. Let's deal with it sometime later. The phala is administered wrt the jiva's previous & the impact of his present karma along with dependent factors like environment, other individuals, freewill, etc.

Thus the Devatas are also Jivas performing their bounden duties till their tenure in the Jagat ends, usually the Manvantara, a measure of time. Now we come to the crux of the point- Paramatma/Ishvara is over & above these overseeing all these activities to it's logical completion of every jiva, an unimaginably complicated process. Here on we can get to see why He is Prabhuh- the King....

'OM Varah namah'

With respect to the Naama 'Sarvaatma' we have briefly discussed the characteristics and relation of all 3 entities in tha world, viz. Jagat, Jiva & Brahma/Ishvara (not to be mistaken with Brahmaa- the Creator). It is evident to a keen mind that all these entities are very different to each other in characteristics & functions. At the outset there are a few similarities owing to the above relation but essentially there seem to be no common attributes or features. For example, we see that Jagat & Ishvara have the Cause-Effect relation & are not different; yet from their functions they are different. Then Jiva & Jagat being interdependent for all functionality, are bound to be entirely different. Thereafter Jiva & Ishvara are similar in some attributes but differ in capabilities. In fact, it is observed that no activity can occur if there are no differences at all. For eg. for energy to flow there is a need for two different potentials- one high & one low. Therefore it is quite confusing for anybody to arrive at a clear understanding to the meaning of this Nama. In other words, we see that all the three relations have a few commonalities but differ in several other aspects. This essentially leads us to deliberate upon the differences & reconcile the purport of the Nama.

Having summarised thus we now take upon how to realise that/arrive at Ishvara is the Atma of everything- Sarvaatma, i.e. how is Ishvara the Atma/essence of the Jagat & how He is the Atma of all Jivas with the help of Shastra, logic/inference, experience. Let us consider the most basic feature of any object animate or otherwise. What could that be? What is the most common feature of everything?

Thinking hard one can say- the reference 'It'. All knowledge & activity is based upon this ubiquitous (all prevalent) understanding. In fact, it is almost never questioned, it is taken for granted.
For eg. all objects are referred to in this manner: "This is a ball", "This is a cow", "It is a horse", "It is technology", "It is science", etc, etc. Further people also are referred to as "This/He is Rama", "This/She is Sita", "This/it is a girl", "This/it is a boy", etc. The common forms in all such statements are "this", "that", "it", "He", "She", "is", etc. Further analysing this usage, everybody refers to himself/herself/itself as "I". For eg. "I walk", "I talk", etc.

Now these references "I", "It" are common to all, irrespective of form or gender, generally coupled with activity or an adjective- "I am this, I am that". The etymology of these words mean "to be". How did this happen? Who taught us this? What is the origin of this understanding/usage?

Ever wonder how all objects can be defined with a simple "It"? How can everybody, inspite of their various activities & differences designate/reference themselves to a simple "I"? How is it so universal to all objects & beings?

Isn't this "I" the inherent feature of Atma/myself? In this Everybody's experience is the same- that "I exist"; That is the Origin. Therefore HE/This "I" (bereft of all references/adjectives) is Sarvaatma. If 'It' is not the same Atma in one & all such a knowledge cannot be universal.

We as an intelligent species, should pause & start/learn to wonder at these seemingly small things in life. It gives us a direction to our path of achievement far from our individualistic attitudes, isn't it? It certainly will lead to a more successful, harmonious & peaceful society.... The Mahabharata.

This identification of the commonness in all objects & beings i.e Ishvara is the essence of (Atma) of everything, is the Advaita jnana propounded by Acharya Shankara, as taught in the Vedas & Upanishats. One who strives & realises this knowledge will surely be at Peace forever, will attain the Supreme. This is the Atma Svarupa. This is the Path to Moksha.

Let us refer to a few statements from the Vedas in this context. The Ishavasya says: 'yastu sarvaani bhootaani atmani eva anupashyati, sarvabhootheshu ca aatmaanam, tato na vijugupsate' meaning The one who sees all the bhootas(forms) in the Atma itself (in Himself); The Atma (Himself) in all the bhootas (forms) then he will transcend the feeling of sadness/remorse. Lord Yama teaches Nachiketas 'Bhidyate hrdaya granthih, chidyante sarva samshayaah, ksheeyante chaasya karmaani tasmin drshte paraavarey' meaning the buddhi (hrdaya) gets unentangled; all doubts, inconsistencies are eliminated, all his karma are destroyed for the one who (learned to) 'see' the Supreme Sthaana of the Atma. Rigveda says ' Tad Vishnoh paramam padam sadaa pashyanti soorayah' meaning The learned see/have in sight that Vishnu's Supreme position always...The Upanishat preaches 'Atmaavaa arey drashtavyah shrothavyo mantavyo nididhidhyasitavyah' meaning Hey This Atma is to be identified/seen, learnt from teaching/hearing, diiscussed/deliberated upon and later assimilated; 'Yo veda nihitam guhayaam parame vyoman. Sah ashnute sarvaan kamaanthsah' meaning He who learns about the One residing in this sacred space of the intellect (guha), will attain all desires alongwith (Jnana). There are innumerable such teachings in the shastras emphasising this Atma is one & the same in all- Sarvaatma.. The famous Rudra prashna is all about this- 'Prostrations to the Ishvara who is the chariot, the charioteer, the sick, the healthy, the sannyasi, the hunter, etc, etc.

'OM Sarvakarah namah'

At the outset, both sarvaatma & sarvah seem to mean one & the same. Of course, it is so. However the subtle difference is in the perspective of the student/sadhaka. Let me explain. When we say He is atma of everything- sarvaatma, the focus of the beginner shifts on to Him- Ishvara, the inward shift towards Atma. When described as Sarvah the focus is on the inevitable activity with/in outward jagat but reminding the sadhaka that the jagat- Namarupas are Ishvara's manifestations; The purpose being the focus back on Ishvara. I might be looking at the web but I actually understand/see the spider- helping the beginners/students' perspective. The other- sarvah, is the baffled sadhakas' perspective. Therefore, we could also see the true purport of the forgone discussion of subjective & objective understanding.

As an example for better understanding lets take the example of arithmetic. In just how many ways can 100 mangoes be counted? Applying rules of math like addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, etc., methods are many- like grouping, counting, etc. The methods used are subjective, the final count of 100 mangoes to be matched/arrived at is objective. Similarly beginning with examples from Jagat being the easiest way for a beginner, Ishvara is Sarvasya Atma. Once identified He is everything; negating the thought of anything different from him.

OM Pravrittih namaH

Pravrittih - Prakarshena kurvadrupataya vartanam Pravrittih is the definition. It means That which exists/remains (vartanam) Especially/emphatically (prakarshena) as the nearest Cause that effects any action (kurvadrupa). In simple words It is the main cause for action i.e The reason that initiates any action. For eg. an advertisement instigates the desire to checkout/acquire a product; a whiplash initiates action in the bullocks. The cry of the infant spurs action in the mother, etc. In Ishvara, as we are already aware, the Pravritti is that of Creation, Sustenance & Destruction of the Jagat. The Samaveda states 'Tajjalaan iti Shanta Upaseeta' meaning Meditate upon Him as the Ever Peaceful & from Whom it (jagat) emanates, disintegrates into and exists in. The Taiitiriya & Chandogya say 'So akamayata, bahusyaam prajaayeya' meaning He (Ishvara) desired thus, ' I shall be born & become many'.

What is the action seen in Lord Shiva?

Apart from the Creation triad, Lord Shiva also oversees the Sthiti- management of the Jagat during it's existence. His actions of Anugraha & Nigraha- Bestowance & Retribution based upon Dharma are all His prowesses which He executes effortlessly. He also manifests as different Avataras for the benefit of the Jivas & provide solace & guidance in the path of Dharma & Salvation apart from saving the worlds from degeneration from time to time.

What is the Cause that instigates such action?

So it is obvious that all these complex activities are performed by the Lord in order to meet the needs of the infinite Jivas in accordance to their previous & present Karma within the framework of Dharma. It means that Ishvara's Pravritti is based on the Jivas' Karma. How is that possible? A parallel can be drawn to explain this: Just as a father makes/builds a toy for his crying child, Ishvara fulfils the Jivas' desires in the ways mentioned above.

Wish to add a note for the sake of clarity: Many people use the argument that The acts of Creation & Delusion, etc. are the God's leela- mirth. It would be wrong to construe that God derives pleasure from/enjoys the discomfiture of His own children/subjects, isn't it? Also the word Leela is also used in the sense 'effortless'. I leave it to your discretion to deliberate upon the point.

However it is true that Ishvara has nothing to achieve from all these activities since He is an Aptakaama (Fulfiled desires). He clearly states that in the Bhagavadgita more than once.

OM Nivritti namaH

Being an Aaptakaama, Shiva has no desires to be fulfilled & is unassociated to any Pravritti. However the karma of ours- Jivas triggers actions in not only us but also in Ishvara, Who otherwise would be in complete serenity, a funny situation of sorts. (Ishvara has to see through the entire process of Creation, etc. in order to fulfil even a single desire of the Jiva). Therefore Shiva is truly of the form Nivritti-'Nikarshena nirudyamatayaa vartanam'. This is His true form- Svarupa.

Another point of explanation is also relevant here. In the framework of Sanatana Dharma there are two paths Pravritti marga & Nivritti marga (B.Gita introduction); also defined as Preyo marga & Shreyo marga (Kathopanishat) respectively. The Pravritti marga consists of all relevant activities within the framework of Dharma for the benefit of all the Jivas. This is framed by Bhagavan Himself so that every Jiva can fulfil his desires & achievements by adhering to & performing their respective stipulated karma. This is borne & propagated by the first born Rishis known as Prajapatis. They are intelectually highly evolved & are born at the beginning of each Creation. Unlike us, they can remember their learnings from previous births in bygone Creations and are Swayambhus (not born of any parents). They simply occur on the earth and continue with the formulation & propagation of Dharma, etc. Swayambhuva Manu, Vasishtha, Atri, Pulastya & many others belong to this Path.

On the other hand there are Rishis Sanatkumaras- Sanaka, Sananandana, Sanatana, Sanatsujata- who renounce the worlds & karmas as soon as they are born & propagate the path of renunciation & Salvation.

They are all immensely well versed in the sciences of the worlds, Dharma & Jnana (almost equal to that of Ishvara); Have tapasya & absolutely blemishless abilities so profound that the Mahabharata advises us to chant & remember their names every morning (Praatasmaraneeyas) if anyone wishes for progress in this life.

Now Ishvara (Shiva) is the Atma of all these Rishis as much as He is ours. It sounds very strange that Shiva is described both as One brimming in activity (Pravritta) and a renunciate (Nivritta) at the same time. This is indeed a very tough aspect to comprehend. However it can be explained as follows:

The purposes of a needle, an axe, an anvil are all different. The benefits & afflictions are limited to their forms, so to say. Their cause-the iron being different from all these forms has no such afflictions. Supposing that the iron is bent, blunt or broken is only denominative (purposeful) to it's forms, iron is said to be unaffected & beyond all this in it's true form.

We Jivas in our wakeful states/rupas/faculties are all different. But we see that in our deep sleep/svarupa we leave behind all our differences & experience the same Oneness & Ananda. It follows that we are certainly different & beyond all of this in our true form. Isn't it true that administerd anaesthesia, one wouldn't feel a thing even if he is hammered, drilled & operated upon (God forbid such a thing). Shantam paapam. Similarly, although Ishvara is a Pravritta or Nivritta in his Vishwaroopa Vaishvanaratma form He is really beyond all of it in all Serenity- 'Shantam Shivam Advaitam Chaturtham Manyante Sa Atma sa vijneyah'.

OM Niyatah namaH

Therefore He is described as Niyatah- One who has rescinded all his faculties; Shiva is the foremost amongst all renunciates. He shines in His true form untouched of- of any Paapa says the Veda- 'Yaa tey Rudra Shivaa tanooraghoraa apaapakashinee...'

Even amongst Jivas it is only possible for one without paapa to truly transcend the bonds of samsara. This is because an iota of desire would somehow germinate into karma which in turn binds & drives one into the cycle of rebirths. Not only that. All actions in the beings are instigated by the indriyas & other faculties listed earlier on- which are themselves activated by desire- Kama. Therefore one who desires to attain the Supreme Shiva has to learn controlling his Indriyas & Karanas. This is explained with a very beautiful analogy in the Kathopanishat. 'Atmaanam rathinam viddhi shariram rathameva tu| Buddhim tu saarathim viddhi manah pragrahameva ca| Indriyani hayaan aahuh vishayans-teshu gocharaan| Atmendriya mano yuktah bhoktaa ityaahuh maneeshinah|' meaning (Lord Yama tells Nachiketas) The Learned say so- Think of yourself (Jiva) as the weary traveller in the chariot, this body being that chariot; Buddhi (intellect) is the charioteer (driving it), the mind is the reins steering it ( the chariot); The Indriyas (karmendriyas & jnanendriyas) are the horses pulling it, the vishayas- objects (& desires) of the worlds are towards which the horses are running forth; Thus this Atma enjoined with the indriyas & manas (karanas) is termed the Bhoktaa (Jnaataa, Kartaa also) by the Intellectuals.

Even to achieve defectless Phalas (results) pertaining to the worlds (Punya) & avoid Paapa one has to have a tremendous amount of alertness & faultless control over the Indriyas, etc. achieved thru Dharma. This has been demonstrated in the examples of Raavana, Hiranyakashipu etc. Asuras; history of many Devas; more evidently in us manushyas. The puranas & histories like Ramayana, Bhaarata are replete with examples & technics to guide us. Therefore, Ishvara is the sole Guru who can guide us in this path, He Himself being the greatest Ascetic. Therefore He is the Real Niyatah residing in all of us. May His grace guide us in the path of Dharma & into Jnana.

The term Dhruva is also understood in the following sense: wherein the spokes of the wheel bind the rim to the axis and the wheel rotates relative to the stationary axis; wherein an absolute co-ordinate is fixed as the origin for all relative & absolute measurements; The metric weight remains fixed relative to all other weighed objects & so forth. In all these measurements- relative or absolute, the reference is always assumed fixed however arbitrary i.e for any measurement to happen the reference has to be & is Dhruva- unmovable.

OM Mahaakarmaa namaH

Mahaakarmaa means He is of the form of all Karmas- Rites & Austerities. The Vedas & Puranas detail many intense & austere Karmas in the form of penance, Yajnas, etc. For eg. Soma yaagas, Paaka yajnas, etc. These complicated Yaagas & Yajnas even span many centuries carried out by continuing progeny.... All this knowledge, capabilities & everything associated with is from & to worship the Lord. 'Yajno vai Vishnuh' meaning Vishnu Himself is Yajna, explain the Vedas... That is, all these Yajnas that are performed are meant to attain Him.

OM Tapasvee namaH

Tapasvee means He is the real One in all austeres....The root tap means to burn, friction, suffer. Living & performing as per the norms of Dharma actually causes a feeling of immense friction & suffering to the performer as well as the associated. Foe eg. Daana. It is very difficult to offer away hard earned wealth & gains obtained thru Dharmic means. It causes great suffering, physically also, to perform austerities like Upavasa-fasting, Jaagarana- keeping awake & being involved in meditation, etc. However all these are meant to obtain & remain in Lord Shiva's oneness. He himself remaining as Jnaana.

The shastras say Realisation of the Self is the highest form of Tapas and the Lord resides unperturbed in it- 'yasya jnaanamayam tapah' says the Upanishat.

* it causes great suffering is used in the sense that it is not easy for any commoner...

OM Bhootabhaavanah namaH

Bhootabhaavanah means from Whom the entire universe manifests. 'yetasmaad aatmana aakaashah sambhootah, aakaashaad vaayuh, vaayoragnih, agneraapah, adbhyah prithivi, prithivyaa oshadhayah, oshadhibhyonnam, annaath purushah' says the Taittiriya explaining the sequence of Creation. Bhoota is the entities of the world; 'bhavanam bhavanaat bhaavanah' existence, to occur/manifest, One who manifests. Therefore it is from Lord Shiva this whole universe & wholeness occurs.
